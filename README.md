After Dark — For Two
A private intimate game for couples

Getting Started
Open after_dark_for_two.html in any browser on your phone or laptop. No internet required after opening — everything runs locally. No accounts, no data stored anywhere outside your device.
The score strip at the top tracks your session across all sections. Names can be saved in the ♡ Us tab.

The Sections
✦ Desires
A prompt drawer with four moods — Sensual, Fantasy, Memory Lane, and Instructions.
How to play:

Pick a mood that matches where you are tonight
Tap Next prompt to draw a desire prompt
Read it out loud or send it as a voice note

Truth & Dare
Five heat levels from 1 (warm) to 5 (on fire). Truths are questions to answer out loud. Dares are things to do — most involve voice notes, video, or sending something.

Tap Done ♡ +1 when complete — this adds to your score

Pick Your Vibe

Tips for the best sessions
Starting a session: Open with the spin wheel (Tonight) or a Desires prompt to set the tone before anything else.
Pace: The sections build naturally — Desires and Truth & Dare work well early, Pick Your Vibe and Just for Us are good for the middle of a session, Toy Play and the Timer work best when you're already warmed up.
The pass-the-screen mechanic: Pick Your Vibe requires passing the phone between partners for each question. Do this literally — one person answers, physically hands the phone to the other. The honesty of not seeing the other person's answer before picking your own is the whole point.
Just for Us privacy: The Her and His sections are genuinely private — one person should step away or the other should look away. The answers are only as honest as the privacy is real.
Hot Seat rule: The no-thinking-time rule is not a suggestion. The value of the Hot Seat is in the unedited first answer. If the person in the seat pauses to think for more than a few seconds, the other person should gently note it.
Bucket List: The solo ticks (things only one of you ticked) are often more interesting than the matches. Use them to open conversations about things that haven't been discussed yet.
Toy Play: Read the tip at the bottom of each idea card — it explains the reasoning behind the task and how to make the most of it. The tips are as important as the tasks themselves.

Privacy
Everything runs locally in your browser. Nothing is sent anywhere. No servers, no accounts, no tracking. Close the tab and the session is gone. The file itself contains all the content — you can save it to a private folder and it will always work offline.
